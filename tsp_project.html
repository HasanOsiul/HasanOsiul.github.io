Section 01

Project Overview

This project explores the Traveling Salesman Problem — a foundational concept from the Management Science classroom. It demonstrates core principles of optimization, route planning, and combinatorial mathematics through interactive visualizations.

"Small improvements in routes can save enormous amounts of time, fuel, and money."
🎯
Optimization

Finding the single best solution from an astronomically large set of possible alternatives. TSP asks: which sequence of visits minimizes total distance?

NP-Hard Min-Cost Exact Methods
🗺️
Route Planning

Determining the most efficient sequence to visit all destinations exactly once and return to the origin. The core of logistics and delivery network design.

Hamiltonian Cycle Real-World Use
📊
Decision Making

Using mathematical models to improve business outcomes. TSP-based solutions power modern delivery apps, airline scheduling, and warehouse management systems.

Data-Driven ROI Impact Cost Savings
🔢
Combinatorial Growth

With just 10 cities, there are over 3.6 million possible routes. At 15 cities: 87 billion. This explosive growth is why optimization algorithms matter so much.

Evidence from Course Work

Supporting materials, data, and visualizations drawn directly from the Management Science classroom activity on the Traveling Salesman Problem.

01
Problem Statement

A salesperson must visit all cities exactly once and return to the starting point while minimizing total travel distance. Formally:

Minimize: Σ d(i,j) · xij
Subject to: each city visited exactly once
Return: to starting city (Hamiltonian cycle)
NP-HardHamiltonian Cycle

What Did I Learn?

A reflection across three dimensions: knowledge gained, skills developed, and personal insights from studying the Traveling Salesman Problem.

🧠
Knowledge
  • The Traveling Salesman Problem and its mathematical foundations
  • Route Optimization strategies and algorithmic approaches
  • Distance Matrices as tools for modeling networks
  • Network Modeling using nodes, edges, and weights
  • Combinatorial Problems — why (n-1)! grows so fast
  • Optimization Techniques: exact, heuristic, and metaheuristic
⚡
Skills
  • Mathematical Modeling — translating real problems into equations
  • Data Analysis — interpreting distance matrices and route data
  • Problem Solving — breaking down NP-Hard challenges
  • Decision Making — selecting optimal solutions under constraints
  • Optimization Thinking — always asking "can we do better?"
  • Visualization Skills — representing networks and routes graphically
💡
Reflection
What Surprised Me
Even a small increase in cities dramatically increases the number of possible routes. Going from 5 to 10 cities multiplies routes by over 15,000×.
What Was Difficult
Understanding that no polynomial-time exact algorithm exists for TSP, and why approximation methods are necessary for large-scale real-world problems.
