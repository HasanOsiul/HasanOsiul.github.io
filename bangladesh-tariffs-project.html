01 / Project Overview

What is This Project About?

A classroom investigation into Bangladesh's unusually high import tariffs — examining which products face the highest taxes, why those tariffs exist, and the real economic consequences for consumers and industries.

📋

Activity Requirements

  • Identify products with extremely high tariffs
  • Investigate why those tariffs exist
  • Analyze economic consequences
  • Evaluate whether the tariffs are reasonable
🏛️

Why Governments Use Tariffs

  • Protect domestic industries from foreign competition
  • Encourage local production
  • Generate government tax revenue
  • Reduce dependence on imports
⚠️

The Downsides of Tariffs

  • Increase prices for consumers
  • Reduce consumer choice in the market
  • Create economic inefficiencies
  • Can slow innovation and slow competition
🌍

Why This Concept Matters

  • Affects everyday food and consumer prices
  • Shapes international trade relationships
  • Impacts business growth and competition
  • Influences national economic development

Every tariff creates winners and losers.

— Core Principle of Trade Policy Economics
Concept Being Demonstrated

Tariffs and Protectionism

A tariff is a tax placed on imported goods at the border. When Bangladesh imposes a tariff on frozen sheep meat or imported coffee, the importing company must pay that tax — and those costs are almost always passed on to consumers in the form of higher prices. This project explores how trade policy decisions made in government offices ripple down to affect everyday shopping and living costs.

Evidence Card 05 · Case Study
☕ The Coffee Tariff Problem

The Problem

Imported specialty coffee faces a 67% tariff in Bangladesh, making a $10 bag of quality coffee cost $16.70 before retailer markups.

Government Rationale

Bangladesh aims to protect its small but growing domestic coffee cultivation industry, particularly in Chittagong hill areas.

The Consequence

Specialty café culture is stunted. High-quality global coffee brands remain inaccessible to most consumers. Café businesses face higher operating costs, limiting growth of the food service sector.

Evidence Card 06
Class Activity Summary

During our classroom investigation, our group evaluated several of Bangladesh's import tariffs — spanning livestock products, seafood, dairy, and beverages. We examined official trade data, discussed government justifications, and debated whether the economic benefits of these tariffs outweigh the costs imposed on consumers.

Our central finding was that while these tariffs serve legitimate policy purposes — protecting domestic industries, generating revenue, and preserving foreign currency — the burden falls disproportionately on consumers, particularly for essential food products like milk powder and meat. We concluded that some tariffs may be reasonable for strategic industries, but others — particularly on basic foodstuffs — may warrant reconsideration given their impact on household affordability.
