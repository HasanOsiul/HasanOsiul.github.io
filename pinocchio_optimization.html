Section 01

Project Overview

Understanding how Linear Programming helps manufacturers allocate limited resources to maximize profit.

🏭
The Activity
This project is based on a classroom activity — The Pinocchio Problem. Pinocchio, Inc. manufactures two types of wooden toys: Trucks and Trains. Management must determine the optimal monthly production plan to maximize profit while respecting labor and demand constraints.
📐
The Concept
The project demonstrates Linear Programming — a mathematical technique that finds the best outcome (maximum profit or minimum cost) from a model whose requirements are represented by linear relationships.
Objective Function Decision Variables Constraints Feasible Region Optimal Solution
📊
Why It Matters
Organizations constantly face limited resources. Managers must decide what to produce, how much to produce, and how to use labor efficiently. LP provides a systematic, mathematical framework for these decisions.
⚙ Manufacturing 📦 Supply Chain 🚚 Logistics 🗓 Scheduling

Section 04

What Did I Learn?

Reflecting on knowledge gained, skills developed, and insights discovered through the Pinocchio optimization problem.

Knowledge

What I Learned

  • Linear Programming as a decision-making framework
  • How to define and interpret Objective Functions
  • The role of Decision Variables in modeling
  • Formulating mathematical Constraints
  • Identifying and graphing the Feasible Region
  • The Corner Point Theorem for finding optima
  • Resource Allocation under scarcity
Skills

What I Developed

  • Mathematical Modeling of real business problems
  • Data Analysis from production spreadsheets
  • Critical Thinking about trade-offs and limits
  • Decision-Making under resource constraints
  • Optimization problem structuring
  • Spreadsheet interpretation and data extraction
  • Systematic Problem Solving methodology
Reflection

My Takeaways

  • A simple toy production problem can be modeled mathematically with precision
  • Constraints fundamentally shape — and often surprise you with — what's optimal
  • The optimal solution always lives on the boundary, never deep inside
  • LP applies everywhere: factories, hospitals, airlines, logistics networks
  • Visual tools like the feasible region make abstract math tangible and meaningful
Real-world applications:
Factory Planning · Manufacturing · Logistics · Business Ops · Supply Chain · Scheduling
