Section 01

Project Overview

How and why countries trade — through the lens of the Gravity Model.

What Is the Gravity Model?

This project explores one of the most important concepts in international trade: the Gravity Model of Trade. Like gravitational force between planets, trade between two countries depends on their "mass" (economic size) and distance from each other.

⚖️ Economic Size 📍 Geographic Distance

How Does It Work?

Countries with large economies tend to trade more because they produce and consume more goods and services. Countries that are geographically closer trade more because transportation costs are lower and business relationships are easier to maintain.

This explains why neighboring countries often become major trading partners and why large economies dominate global trade networks.

🌍 Why This Matters

Understanding trade patterns helps governments, businesses, and economists make better decisions about international commerce, investment, and economic development. The Gravity Model provides a reliable framework for predicting and analyzing real-world trade flows.

Section 04

Key Concepts

The four pillars that underpin the Gravity Model of Trade.

⚖️

Economic Size

Larger economies generate greater demand and production, creating more opportunities for exports and imports with partner nations.

📍

Distance

Greater geographic distance increases transportation and logistics costs, acting as a natural barrier that reduces trade volumes between countries.

🤝

Trade Relationships

Neighboring countries naturally develop deeper trade ties due to shared cultural connections, established logistics routes, and lower transaction costs.

🌐

Globalization

Advances in technology and transportation have reduced the friction of distance, expanding trade networks — though geographic proximity still matters significantly.

Section 05 — Reflection

What Did I Learn?

Reflecting across knowledge, skills, and personal insight gained through this project.

Knowledge

What I Discovered

I learned that trade flows are systematically influenced by both economic size and geographic distance. The Gravity Model provides a quantitative framework that helps explain why some countries trade far more than others — and predicts future trade patterns with remarkable accuracy.

Skills

What I Developed

I developed skills in economic analysis, interpreting trade data, and understanding international market relationships. I also practiced creating visual and interactive representations of economic concepts to make complex ideas more accessible.

Reflection

Surprising Insights

Before studying international trade, I assumed globalization had made geographic distance largely irrelevant. This project revealed that physical location still plays a major role in shaping trade relationships — even in our hyper-connected world.

Conclusion

Why the Gravity Model Matters

The Gravity Model remains one of the most powerful and empirically validated tools for explaining real-world trade patterns. By combining just two variables — economic size and distance — it provides extraordinary predictive power about how countries interact within the global economy. As trade policies evolve and new economic powers emerge, this model continues to guide economists, policymakers, and businesses worldwide.
