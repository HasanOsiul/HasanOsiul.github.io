9:00 — 11:30 AM

Daewangam Park 대왕암공원

Probably the most beautiful place in Ulsan — and the morning's anchor.

A black pine forest opens onto a jagged volcanic coastline, where a suspension bridge sways out over the open sea toward a lighthouse rock. Slow paths, sea air, and the best light of the day for photographs.

11:30 AM — 12:30 PM

Ilsan Beach 일산해수욕장

A short walk from Daewangam, and the day's first real pause.

Soft sand, calm shallow water, and a wide promenade backed by low hills. No itinerary item here — just slow down, feel the sea breeze, and let the morning's walking settle before lunch.

12:30 — 1:30 PM

Lunch near Ilsan Beach

Fresh seafood is the obvious move this close to the water — raw fish, grilled shellfish, or a spicy seafood stew. If you'd rather not, the side streets behind the beach have solid Korean standards too.

2:00 — 4:30 PM

Taehwagang National Garden 태화강 국가정원

One of Korea's best urban parks — and a complete change of pace.

A vast bamboo forest, locally called Sipri Daenamu-sup, lines the river for nearly a kilometer, with paths that turn the afternoon light green. Beyond it, open gardens and river views make this the day's most peaceful stretch.

Huge bamboo forest

5:00 — 6:00 PM

Ulsan Grand Park 울산대공원

The fountain, finally — timed for the day's best light.

As the sun drops, the park's musical fountain show begins (schedule varies by season), with jets lit gold and rose against the evening sky. The whole park is built for an evening walk, but this is the moment to slow down and watch.

Sunset photos
Evening walk

6:00 — 7:00 PM

Dinner in Ulsan

Wind down near the city center or back around the Taehwagang area — both have plenty of options for a relaxed final meal before the train.

7:30 — 8:00 PM

Return to Busan

Train from Taehwagang Station back toward Busan. Arrive around 8:30–9:00 PM — a full day, coast to garden to fountain, and home by night.
